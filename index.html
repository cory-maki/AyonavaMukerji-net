The Story Behind the Work

From the Tools to the Top

Shupi Mukerji started where most great builders start — as a carpenter. He completed his Certificate III through the CFMEU apprenticeship scheme, becoming one of only two participants from that programme to go on to found his own construction business. That foundation — trade skills paired with trade ethics — shapes everything he does at Omega Structures today.

Over the years, Shupi advanced through senior roles at Hutchinson Builders, Wideform, and Caelli Formwork, sharpening his ability to run large-scale projects without losing sight of the details that make or break a site. His is a career built on consistency: the same standard every day, regardless of who is watching.

When the Queensland government drafted the Formwork Code of Practice 2016, Shupi was at the table — a practitioner's voice in a process that too often excludes the people closest to the work. That contribution defines his broader philosophy: industry improves when the people doing the job shape its rules.
