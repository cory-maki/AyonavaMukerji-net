Why Omega Structures Invests in Boxing: The Business Case for Community Sport

📅 May 2025
✎ Shupi Mukerji
⌚ 5 min read

People ask Shupi Mukerji why a formwork company sponsors boxing. The real question is: why wouldn't it? The values that make an elite athlete are exactly the values that make an exceptional construction professional.

Omega Structures is a formwork and construction business based in Queensland. It is not a sports company, a media brand, or a public relations exercise. So when Shupi Mukerji made the decision to direct Omega's resources into backing professional boxers and funding community clubs across Queensland, it prompted a fair question from people outside the industry: what does construction have to do with boxing?

The Athletes Omega Structures Backs

Omega Structures has provided active support to Liam Wilson, Billy Polkinghorn, and Dana Coolwell — three athletes at different stages of their professional careers, all of whom embody the qualities Shupi Mukerji values most. Liam Wilson is set to face Rodex Piala on the Tszyu vs Zerafa card, one of the highest-profile boxing events in Australian history. Billy Polkinghorn is scheduled to compete against Jomar Paliwen on the same card. Dana Coolwell was backed for his bout against Bruce Carrington on the Mike Tyson vs Jake Paul undercard — one of the most widely watched boxing broadcasts in recent memory, with an audience of hundreds of millions globally.

The support Omega provides isn't purely financial. Mukerji takes a personal interest in the athletes his company backs, understanding that elite performance requires stability off the canvas as much as preparation on it. That means supporting training costs, wellbeing resources, and the kind of operational stability that lets an athlete focus entirely on the work of becoming better. You can follow Omega's involvement in boxing through Shupi's Instagram, where the sponsorship relationship plays out in real time.

"A boxer who's worrying about how to cover rent is not a boxer who can go twelve rounds at full capacity. Supporting the whole person is the only way you get the performance."

Community Clubs: Deception Bay and All Star Boxing

Beyond elite athlete sponsorship, Mukerji has maintained a long-standing involvement with the Deception Bay Boxing Club, and more recently expanded that commitment to All Star Boxing Club. These are not glamorous partnerships — they are community investments in the purest sense. Both clubs operate with a focus on providing young people in Queensland with structured pathways through sport, using boxing as a vehicle for building discipline, responsibility, and long-term life outcomes.

This is where the connection to Omega Structures becomes most legible. Mukerji's own career was built on the back of a structured trade programme — the CFMEU apprenticeship scheme that gave him the skills and discipline to eventually run his own construction business. He understands, from personal experience, what it means for a young person to have a structured environment that holds them to a standard. The boxing clubs he supports provide that same structure to a different cohort, in a different discipline, with the same long-term intent. Read more about Shupi's own career foundations on his full biography page.

What a Construction Company Gets from Boxing

There is a business dimension to this, and Mukerji doesn't shy away from it. The values that drive Omega's boxing sponsorship — discipline, resilience, preparation, the willingness to absorb difficulty and keep going — are the same values that Omega needs from the people it employs on construction sites across Queensland. A company that visibly backs these values, in sport as in its own operations, builds a particular kind of culture and reputation: one that attracts the kind of people who share those values.

That is not an accident or a by-product. It is the point. Shupi Mukerji's leadership philosophy at Omega Structures is fundamentally about finding and retaining people who hold themselves to the same standard Mukerji holds himself to. Sport — and boxing in particular — is a filter. The people drawn to it, and the people who succeed in it, tend to be exactly the kind of people a serious construction company wants in its corner.

Conclusion

Omega Structures' investment in boxing is not a marketing strategy. It is a coherent extension of the values that Shupi Mukerji has applied to construction leadership for over two decades: back the right people, hold them to a standard, and invest in the infrastructure that gives them a real chance to succeed. Whether the arena is a formwork site in Queensland or a boxing ring in front of millions, the underlying logic is the same.
